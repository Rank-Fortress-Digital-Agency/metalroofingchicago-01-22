How Metal Roofs Work

Metal roofs work differently than traditional asphalt shingle roofs. Most asphalt shingles are made of felt paper with an asphalt coating to create water resistance. Asphalt is not very durable, but it is very inexpensive and easy to install. Unfortunately, these roofs have a relatively short lifespan because the adhesive used to bond the layers together breaks down over time.

Asphalt shingles have a lifespan of about 25 years – if you’re lucky – so homeowners have to spend thousands every decade or so just to keep their roofs intact.

Metal roofs, on the other hand, are made of interlocking panels that are bent into shape. Metal roofs can be installed in less than one day and they come with a warranty of up to 50 years. This means you won’t have to spend thousands every decade or so just to keep your roof intact. Instead, you’ll have to spend thousands every half-century! If you live somewhere where it snows in the wintertime, having a metal snow roof is even better because snow weighs approximately 12 pounds per cubic foot. Snow will easily slide right off your roof instead of being forced under shingles by wind gusts – which might eventually cause leaks inside your house.

Climates Where Metal Roofs Thrive

As we’ve already mentioned, metal roofs live for a very long time. There is some debate over just how long they will last in different climates, but most estimates say that metal roofs can survive about 80-90 years in snowy, icy regions and about 70-80 years in sunny hot areas.

Metal roofs are built to last so you won’t need to replace them as often as traditional asphalt shingle roofs, but there are still many factors that contribute to their lifespan. For example, Metal roofs might not do well if they don’t receive enough sunlight because paneling needs radiation from the sun to dry out the inside of the roof over time. If water isn’t allowed to escape through evaporation then it could pool underneath your roof and start to cause problems.

Metal roofs are a great choice for homeowners who live in snowy regions or sunny areas with high heat, but they might not be right for people living somewhere where it snows only occasionally or somewhere that experiences freezing temperatures year-round. Additionally, metal roofs can become very hot during warm weather so if you’re planning on putting your roofing panels in direct sunlight then you should consider installing an overhang to protect them from the elements.

Energy Efficiency

The installation of metal roofs over traditional asphalt shingles will increase the R-value of your entire roof system, making it much more difficult for heat to escape in the summer months. This means that heating and cooling costs will be lower than they would be with other types of roofing materials.


Durability / Longevity

Metal material has extremely good weather resistance properties, making it a great choice due to its ability to handle harsh climates. Another major benefit is that when installed properly, a metal roof can last up to 50 years or more.

Noise Reduction

Metal roofs are great at dampening noise that usually penetrates your home through the ceiling. This is due to the mass of the material and its ability to absorb it without transferring it inside where you live. Metal roofs also help make homes quieter by deflecting wind over them versus traditional asphalt materials which tend to whistle in high winds.
